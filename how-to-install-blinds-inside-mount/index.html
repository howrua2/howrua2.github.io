The Challenges of Installing Blinds Inside Mount

Installing blinds inside mount requires more precision than outside mount installation. Inside mount blinds will need to fit within the recessed space of your window frame. As a result, it can be tricky to measure properly. You’ll also need to be aware of any obstructions, such as window locks or handles, that may affect the fit of your blinds.

How to Install Blinds Inside Mount

The first step in installing blinds inside mount is to measure the width and height of your window opening. Make sure to measure in three places, the top, middle, and bottom, to ensure a precise fit. Subtract 1/4 inch from each measurement to ensure your blinds fit within the window opening without rubbing or binding.

Next, attach the brackets to the top of your window frame using screws and a level to ensure they are straight. Ensure that the brackets are parallel to each other and level to prevent the blinds from hanging crooked.

Once the brackets are in place, insert the headrail of the blinds into the brackets. Ensure that the blinds are level before tightening the screws to secure them. Finally, insert the included valance clips into the headrail and snap the valance into place for a finished look.

Summary of the Main Points

When installing blinds inside mount, precision is key. Measure your window opening carefully, ensure the brackets are level, and take your time when inserting the headrail of the blinds. Once installed, your blinds should fit snugly within the recessed space of your window frame, providing a professional, polished look.

Tips for Overcoming Challenges in Blind Installation

When installing blinds inside mount, it’s important to pay attention to any obstructions that may interfere with the fit of the blinds. If needed, you may need to remove window locks or handles to ensure a precise fit. Be sure to use a level when installing the brackets to ensure that your blinds hang evenly.

Adjusting the Fit of Inside Mount Blinds

If your blinds are too wide or too long for your window opening, don’t fret. There are a few simple adjustments you can make to ensure a proper fit. First, remove any excess slats by removing the bottom rail and cutting the excess length with a hacksaw or pruning shears. If your blinds are too wide, you can also remove one or more slats from the headrail using a pair of pliers.

Maintaining Inside Mount Blinds

To keep your inside mount blinds looking great for years to come, it’s important to clean them regularly. Use a soft cloth or a vacuum cleaner attachment to remove dust and debris from the slats and valance. Avoid using water or harsh chemicals, which can damage the finish or warp the slats.

Question and Answer

Q: How do I know if I need inside mount or outside mount blinds?

A: If your window frame has enough depth, we recommend inside mount blinds for a polished look. Outside mount blinds are a good option if your window opening is not deep enough to accommodate inside mount blinds.

Q: Can I install blinds inside mount on my own, or should I hire a professional?

A: While installing blinds inside mount can be a bit tricky, it is definitely doable for the average homeowner. However, if you are uncomfortable with the process or have more complex windows, it may be worth hiring a professional to install your blinds.

Q: How do I adjust the slats on my inside mount blinds?

A: To adjust the slats on your inside mount blinds, simply tilt them up or down using the wand or cord. You can also adjust the amount of light that enters the room by lifting or lowering the blinds using the cord or wand.

Q: Will inside mount blinds offer the same level of privacy as outside mount blinds?

A: Yes, inside mount blinds can provide the same level of privacy as outside mount blinds, as long as they are properly installed and fit snugly within the window opening.

Conclusion of How to Install Blinds Inside Mount

Installing blinds inside mount may seem daunting, but with a bit of patience and precision, it is definitely doable. Pay attention to any obstructions that may interfere with the fit, use a level to ensure that your blinds hang evenly, and take your time when inserting the headrail of the blinds. With a little effort, your inside mount blinds will provide a polished, professional look in your home for years to come.
