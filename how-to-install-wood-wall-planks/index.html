If you’re looking to add some character and warmth to a room, installing wood wall planks is a great option. Not only do they provide a rustic look, but they can also add value to your home. However, the installation process may seem daunting to some. Don’t worry, we’ve got you covered. In this article, we’ll walk you through how to install wood wall planks.

When it comes to installing wood wall planks, some common pain points include the fear of damaging the walls, the time and effort required, and the cost of materials and tools. But with the right tools and techniques, these pain points can be easily addressed.

How to Install Wood Wall Planks

First, decide on the type of wood and the pattern you want to use for your wall. You can choose from different types of wood such as reclaimed, birch, pine, and cedar, to name a few. Once you’ve decided on the type of wood, buy or rent the necessary tools, including a saw, level, drill, nails, and adhesive. Measure the wall and mark the studs with the help of a stud finder tool.

Next, you’ll want to cut the planks to the desired length and remove any defects that may affect the aesthetic. Then, sand the planks to a smooth finish. Once you have your planks ready, start at the bottom of the wall and work your way up. Use adhesive directly on the plank or on the wall, and then nail them to the wall, making sure to hit the studs. Use a level to ensure that the planks are straight. Stagger the seams to create a more natural look.

In summary, installing wood wall planks requires selecting the type of wood and pattern, measuring the wall and marking the studs, cutting the planks, using adhesive and nails to secure them to the wall, and staggering the seams.

I used reclaimed wood and staggered the seams, which gave the wall a cozy and natural look. Overall, I’m very happy with how it turned out.

Tips for Installing Wood Wall Planks in Corners

Installing wood wall planks in corners can be tricky, but with a few tips, the process can be smoother. One tip is to miter the corners, which means cutting the ends of two planks at a 45-degree angle so that they fit together when placed in the corner. Another tip is to use corner trim to cover up any small gaps that may be left.

### Using a Level to Install Wood Wall Planks

Using a level is crucial to ensure that the wood planks are installed straight. If the planks aren’t level, it can create an uneven and unprofessional look. To use a level, place it on top of the plank and adjust until it’s straight. You can also use a laser level for more accuracy.

#### Maintaining and Cleaning Wood Wall Planks

Proper maintenance and cleaning of wood wall planks can help to extend their lifespan and keep them looking great. Regular dusting and vacuuming can help to remove any debris that may accumulate on the planks. You can also use a damp cloth to wipe down the planks, but avoid using too much water, which can cause the wood to warp or expand. When it comes to maintenance, it’s important to inspect the planks regularly for any signs of damage or wear and tear.

Personal Experience with Wood Wall Planks Maintenance

After installing wood wall planks in my bedroom, I noticed that they needed some occasional maintenance to keep them looking great. I made sure to regularly dust and vacuum them, and also used a damp cloth to wipe them down. I also inspected them regularly and fixed any damage that I noticed to ensure that they last for many years.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need to remove the baseboard trim before installing wood wall planks?

A: It’s not necessary to remove the baseboard trim, but it may make the installation process easier. If you choose to leave the trim in place, you’ll need to make sure that the planks are installed flush against the trim to prevent any gaps.

Q: Can I install wood wall planks over wallpaper?

A: It’s not recommended to install wood wall planks over wallpaper. The wallpaper may prevent the planks from adhering properly to the wall and can cause the planks to become loose over time.

Q: Do I need to acclimate the wood planks before installation?

A: It’s a good idea to acclimate the wood planks to the room’s temperature and humidity levels before installation. This can help to prevent any warping or buckling of the planks after installation.

Q: What is the average cost of installing wood wall planks?

A: The cost of installing wood wall planks can vary depending on the type of wood, the size of the wall, and the tools needed. On average, the cost can range from $5 to $10 per square foot.
