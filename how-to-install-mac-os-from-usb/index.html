How to Create a Bootable USB Drive

Creating a bootable USB drive is the first step in installing macOS from USB. To do this, you’ll need a 16GB or larger USB flash drive and access to a Mac. You’ll also need a copy of the macOS installer, which you can obtain from the Mac App Store. Once you have all the necessary components, you can start creating your bootable USB drive.

Firstly, connect your USB flash drive to your Mac. Open Disk Utility and select your USB drive from the list of available disks. Format the USB drive as Mac OS Extended (Journaled), and give it a name (e.g., “MacInstall”). Next, open Terminal and copy and paste the following command:

sudo /Applications/Install\ macOS\ High\ Sierra.app/Contents/Resources/createinstallmedia --volume /Volumes/MacInstall --applicationpath /Applications/Install\ macOS\ High\ Sierra.app --nointeraction

Replace “Install\ macOS\ High\ Sierra.app” with the name of your macOS installer, and “MacInstall” with the name you gave your USB drive. Press Enter to run the command, and wait for the process to complete. Once done, your USB drive is bootable and contains a copy of the macOS installer.

Installing macOS from USB

Now that you have a bootable USB drive, you’re ready to install macOS from USB. To do this, plug your USB drive into your Mac, restart your Mac, and hold down the Option key while your Mac boots up. This will take you to the boot manager, where you can select your USB drive to boot from.

Once you’ve selected your USB drive, the macOS installer will launch. Follow the on-screen instructions to install macOS on your Mac. You’ll be asked to select your language, and then the installation process will begin. Depending on your Mac’s configuration, the installation process may take some time to complete. Once the process is complete, your Mac will restart, and you’ll be able to use the latest version of macOS.
