Transforming the look of your home’s exterior can be a major undertaking, but installing cedar shake siding is well worth the effort. Not only does it add warmth and character to your home’s facade, but cedar is also a durable, natural material that requires little maintenance. In this article, we will go over the steps you need to take to install cedar shake siding and ensure that your home looks its best for years to come.

Potential Challenges

Before diving into the installation process, it’s important to consider some potential challenges you may face when installing cedar shake siding. Firstly, working with cedar shake can be tricky, as it requires specialized tools and techniques to install correctly. Secondly, because it is a natural material, cedar can be susceptible to rot and insects if it is not properly installed and maintained. Finally, while cedar shake is relatively low-maintenance, it may require additional upkeep compared to other siding materials.

How to Install Cedar Shake Siding

To ensure that your cedar shake siding is installed properly and will last for years to come, we recommend following these steps:

  1. Remove any existing siding, if necessary
  2. Install a moisture barrier, like house wrap or building paper
  3. Install a starter strip at the bottom of the wall
  4. Install the first row of cedar shake, making sure each piece overlaps the previous one by at least 1 inch
  5. Continue installing rows, ensuring that each piece is straight and level
  6. Trim any edges as necessary, using specialized tools like a cedar shake cutter or circular saw
  7. Seal the siding with a high-quality sealant or stain to protect it from the elements

Summary

Installing cedar shake siding requires specialized tools and techniques, but when done correctly, it can add character and warmth to your home’s exterior. To ensure that your cedar shake siding lasts for years to come, it’s important to consider potential challenges like rot and insects, and to follow the proper installation steps, including removing existing siding, installing a moisture barrier, and sealing the siding with a high-quality sealant or stain.

Preparation Tips

Before you can start installing your cedar shake siding, it’s important to properly prepare your work area. This includes ensuring that your walls are clean and free of any debris or damage. Additionally, you should make sure you have all the necessary tools and materials before you start, including a ladder, cedar shake cutter, and high-quality sealant or stain. For best results, we recommend enlisting the help of a professional contractor with experience installing cedar shake siding.

Benefits of Cedar Shake Siding

Cedar shake siding is a popular choice for homeowners due to its natural beauty and durability. Along with being low-maintenance, cedar is also resistant to insects and rot, making it a smart investment for homeowners looking to increase the value of their property. Additionally, cedar shake siding can help improve your home’s energy efficiency by providing extra insulation and reducing heat loss.

Installation Tips

When installing cedar shake siding, it’s important to choose the right type of cedar for your project. We recommend using western red cedar, which is more durable and resistant to insects than other types of cedar. Additionally, you should take care to properly space your cedar shake pieces, leaving a small gap between each piece to allow for expansion and contraction with changes in temperature and moisture levels.

Question and Answer

Q: How long does cedar shake siding typically last?

A: With proper installation and maintenance, cedar shake siding can last up to 30 years or more.

Q: What is the best way to clean cedar shake siding?

A: To clean cedar shake siding, we recommend using a solution of mild detergent and water, and gently scrubbing with a soft-bristled brush. Avoid using harsh chemicals or power washers, as these can damage the wood.

Q: Can cedar shake siding be painted?

A: While cedar shake siding can be painted, it is not the most recommended option as it requires regular maintenance. Instead, most homeowners choose to seal or stain their cedar shake siding to maintain its natural beauty and durability.

Q: Is cedar shake siding more expensive than other siding materials?

A: Cedar shake siding is typically more expensive than other siding materials like vinyl or fiber cement. However, because it is a natural material, it can add value to your home and provide long-term cost savings by reducing the need for frequent repairs or replacements.

Conclusion of How to Install Cedar Shake Siding

Installing cedar shake siding can be a rewarding project that adds character and warmth to your home’s exterior. By following the proper installation steps and taking care to properly maintain your siding, you can ensure that it lasts for years to come and provides long-term value for your property.
