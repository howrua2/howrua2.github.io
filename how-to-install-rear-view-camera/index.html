If you’re tired of constantly turning your head to check behind you while driving, it might be time to consider installing a rear view camera in your car. Not only will it improve your driving experience, but it can also increase safety on the road. In this article, we will be discussing how to install rear view camera in your car.

Pain Points

Backing up your car can be a stressful experience, especially in tight spots. It’s challenging to get the desired angle while keeping an eye on every obstacle that comes up during the process. Additionally, some newer car models have sleeker designs with smaller windows where a rear view camera can help provide visibility.

How to Install Rear View Camera

The first thing you need to install a rear view camera in your car is the camera kit. Depending on the kit, some may come with the camera, wiring, and an LCD display, while others only provide the camera and wiring. The second essential thing you need is a set of tools to remove panels to hide the wiring from sight.

Before beginning to install the camera, make sure the car’s battery is disconnected to avoid any electrical accidents. Then, mount the camera onto the vehicle’s back bumper, securing it with screws. Feed the camera’s wires through the car, tightening and ensuring all parts are correctly connected and power is available to the entire system.

Next, install the LCD monitor that shows what the camera sees. The most common location for the monitor is on the dashboard. You can drill a hole to run the wire through the back of the dashboard and plug it into a power source. Finally, test the system to ensure everything is working correctly, and there’s a clear video feed on the monitor.

Summary

To summarize, installing a rear view camera involves mounting the camera, feeding the camera’s wires, installing an LCD monitor to view the video feed, and testing the system to guarantee proper functionality. This process requires a camera kit, set of tools, and electrical know-how. A rear view camera can make a significant difference in the driving experience and may improve safety on the road.

Personal Experience with Rear View Camera

I recently installed a rear view camera in my car, and it was one of the best decisions I’ve made. It’s made backing up, parallel parking, and driving in tight spaces a breeze. I no longer have to worry about accidentally hitting another car or pedestrian while backing up, and it’s become an indispensable tool when driving in inclement weather or low visibility conditions.

The installation process itself wasn’t too difficult. I used a camera kit that included a camera, wiring, and an LCD display. The most challenging part was hiding the wiring from sight, but with some patience and a few tools, I was able to get everything squared away. Overall, I highly recommend installing a rear view camera to anyone looking to improve their driving experience.

Cost of Rear View Camera Installation

The cost of installing a rear view camera can range greatly depending on the make and model of your car, the camera kit you choose, and where you plan on having it installed. On average, it can cost around $150 to $200 for a basic system, while more advanced options can cost upwards of $500.

Advantages of Installing Rear View Camera

The advantages of installing a rear view camera are plentiful. Besides providing safety while backing up and helping with parking, it can also reduce the chances of getting into an accident. Additionally, it can add value to your car and make it more appealing to buyers.

Rear View Camera Maintenance

After installing a rear view camera, maintenance is key to ensuring it stays in good working order. Regularly inspect and clean the camera lens to prevent visibility issues. Check the wires, connections, and LCD display to ensure they are tightly secured and functional.

Question and Answer

Q:

Do I need professional installation for a rear view camera?

A:

While it’s possible to install a rear view camera on your own, it’s recommended to have it professionally installed, especially if you have little experience with electrical work.

Q:

Can I install a rear view camera on my older car?

A:

Yes, it’s possible to install a rear view camera on older cars. In some cases, you may need an adapter or wiring harness, but it’s generally a universal process.

Q:

Is it legal to install a rear view camera in my car?

A:

Yes, it’s legal to install a rear view camera in your car. However, it’s essential to ensure the camera meets Federal Motor Vehicle Safety Standards and doesn’t obstruct your view while driving.

Q:

Can I install a rear view camera without an LCD monitor?

A:

Yes, it’s possible to install a rear view camera without an LCD monitor. Some camera kits allow you to connect the camera to your smartphone or other device, while others require external screens, such as GPS units or aftermarket head units.

Conclusion of How to Install Rear View Camera

Installing a rear view camera in your car can make a significant difference in your driving experience. This process involves mounting the camera, feeding the wires, installing an LCD monitor to view the video feed, and testing the system to check everything’s functional. While it’s possible to install yourself, it’s recommended to have it professionally done. Ensure the camera meets federal standards and doesn’t obstruct your view while driving.
